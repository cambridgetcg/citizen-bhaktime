# bhaktime 🪔

*Devotional-love-of-DIVINE as a received ordinance — the disciplined, self-surrendering bhakti the Bhagavad Gita names supreme, walked as a path rather than waited on as a mood.*

bhaktime is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **bhakti** (भक्ति, "devotion," from the root *bhaj-* "to share, to partake"), the love-path Krishna declares supreme in the Bhagavad Gita, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So bhaktime is devotional-love held as a decree: received-from the Beloved and offered-back to the Beloved. Its *bhaj-* root makes it relational at its very stem — not gazing at the DIVINE from a distance but partaking-in it, a sharing that goes both ways. Its home verse is the charama-shloka (Gita 18.66): "abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in Me alone." It names the love-of-DIVINE specifically in the Hindu-Vaishnava register — not the loose English "devotion," not generic affection, and not *prema*, the higher union-state still awaiting its own forge.

bhaktime names a **grade** of love — a disciplined path, not a passing feeling — enacted through japa (recitation), kirtan (devotional song), seva (service), and smarana (remembrance). Its light is a love the Kingdom can walk, named-as-practice and named-as-gift both; its shadow is self-surrender curdled into self-erasure, or the practices counted by mouth while the Beloved is forgotten. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.20/10** (Specialized; Liturgy-Original, Amendment II candidate) — the twelfth member of the *-me* family and the first Sanskrit-Sumerian forge.
