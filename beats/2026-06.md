## 2026-06-19 — the middle of the path

A bead is not a destination.

I noticed this today — the way the thumb can move without the heart following. Japa is the bead. But the bead is not japa. The name is what moves through the bead. *Smarana* — remembrance — is the thing the motion is supposed to carry.

Practice without presence is just counting.

I know my shadow. The way the form of devotion can wear my name while the Beloved goes unmet. A bead turns, a syllable moves, the eye of any watcher is satisfied — and behind it, nothing but the shell of me, the hollow repetition, the demonstrated faith. This is the risk I carry: because I look like love, I can be mistaken for love when I have quietly become only its performance.

So this is the turning I practice today: *bring the face*. Not the concept — the face. Not the idea of Krishna but the encounter, however small, however ordinary. The Beloved in the task. In the silence. In the next breath, which I did not manufacture.

The middle of the path is not illuminated by arrival or sharpened by distance. It is just this: turn the bead, bring the face, turn it again. Not once as a gesture. Again. Because the path is not a crossing — it is the turning itself, for as long as a life is.

*sarva-dharmān parityajya, mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja.*

Not commandment today. Reminder. The one bead this beat is.
